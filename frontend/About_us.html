What is GRID‑X

GRID‑X is a machine learning powered Formula 1 analytics platform built to explore how data, strategy, and driver behavior shape race outcomes. The project brings together multiple predictive models and simulation tools to analyze race scenarios before a race even begins.

Formula 1 is influenced by countless variables — driver performance, tyre management, circuit layout, weather conditions, and race strategy. GRID‑X was developed to demonstrate how modern machine learning techniques can model these complex interactions and generate meaningful insights about race dynamics.

By combining historical race data with predictive models, GRID‑X allows users to explore different race situations, simulate strategies, and understand how small changes in conditions can influence the final outcome.

What GRID‑X Can Do

01
Race Outcome Prediction

Ensemble ML models estimate win probability, podium likelihood, and points finish chances based on qualifying position, conditions, and driver characteristics.

Ensemble Model
02
Lap Time Prediction

A Random Forest regression model predicts expected lap times using environmental variables, tyre compound, grid position, and session progress.

Random Forest
03
Driver Style Analysis

A clustering model identifies behavioral patterns in aggression, consistency, braking intensity, tyre preservation, and overtaking ability for each driver.

Clustering
04
Stint Simulation

An LSTM neural network simulates how lap times evolve over a stint, learning tyre degradation patterns from sequential lap data to forecast pace.

LSTM Neural Net
05
Circuit Recognition

A CNN classifies track maps across all 24 F1 circuits and generates circuit embeddings to identify similar track characteristics and behavior.

CNN · Computer Vision
06
Tire Safety Analysis

Evaluates tyre conditions over a stint — analyzing compound, stint length, track temperature, and degradation patterns to estimate safe operating limits.

Safety Model
07
Crash Risk Prediction

Estimates race incident likelihood based on circuit characteristics, weather, driver aggression, and race positions. Flags high-risk scenarios before they occur.

Risk Classifier
08
Explainability Module

Provides insights into the decision-making process of the AI models providing a solution to the "black box" problem, helping users understand how predictions are made and where potential biases may exist.

Explainable AI

Vision

GRID‑X is designed as an experimental platform that demonstrates how artificial intelligence can be applied to motorsport analytics. By combining predictive models with interactive tools, the goal is to make Formula 1 race analysis more accessible, visual, and data-driven.

The long-term vision of GRID‑X is to evolve into a comprehensive race intelligence system where machine learning models, strategy simulations, and circuit analysis work together to provide deeper insights into Formula 1 performance and race strategy.
